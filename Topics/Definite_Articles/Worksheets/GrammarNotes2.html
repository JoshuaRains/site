Definite Articles in Spanish

Remember that in Spanish, definite articles are used to talk about specific nouns. They are the equivalent of the word "the" in English.

Unlike in English, Spanish definite articles must agree with the gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural) of the noun they describe.

Vocabulary sheets will typically include the definite articles for each noun because they make it easy to recognize the gender of confusing words.

Here are the four definite articles in Spanish:

  • el - masculine, singular (el libro - the book)
  • la - feminine, singular (la mesa - the table)
  • los - masculine, plural (los libros - the books)
  • las - feminine, plural (las mesas - the tables)
