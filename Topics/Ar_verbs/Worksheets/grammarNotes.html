-ar Verbs in Spanish
Verbs are words that show

In Spanish, the base forms of verbs will always have one of 3 different endings: -ar, -er, or -ir. These base forms are called infinitives. They are the equivelent of having "to" in front of a verb in English. Here are some examples:

caminar - to walk
correr - to run
abrir - to open

Today we are just going to focus on how to use -ar verbs like caminar.

When a subject is doing the action of the verb, we have to change the ending of the verb to match that subject. This process is called conjugation.

Present Tense Endings for -ar Verbs:

To conjugate, there are 2 steps:

  • remove the -ar
  • add the new ending (one of the following: -o, -as, -a, -amos, -áis, -an)
