Ser or Estar?

Estar means "to be" in certain contexts. As you already learned, the verb Ser also means "to be." So when do you use one instead of the other?

Here are their conjugations for reference:

There are two acronyms that can help us determine which one to use. D.O.C.T.O.R and P.L.A.C.E

This can take some getting used to, but these will help you determine which verb you need to use depending on what the sentence is talking about. We will do some practice.
